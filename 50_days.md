⭐ PART 1 — Why Giving Mock Tests is NON-NEGOTIABLE
Don’t skip mocks even if your marks are low.

Mock tests help you improve:

Time management

Accuracy

Exam temperament

Negative marking reduction

Without mocks, the real exam will feel unfamiliar.

⭐ PART 2 — Should you start full tests now?
If syllabus 80% or more complete → Start full-length tests immediately.
If syllabus 30–40% complete →

First finish 1–2 more subjects

Try to reach 70% completion by Jan 10

Then start full tests

⭐ PART 3 — The Most Important Section: Paper Attempt Strategy

The speaker explains the exact strategy he used to get AIR 1 twice.

🔵 STRATEGY STEP 1 — DO NOT Scroll the Entire Paper

A common wrong suggestion: "Scroll all 65 questions first."

This wastes 10 minutes upfront.
NEVER do this.

🔵 STRATEGY STEP 2 — Classify Each Question into 3 Types
TYPE A: Easy Question

Seen in class or PYQs

Formula-based

You immediately understand it

👉 Solve immediately

TYPE B: Known but Lengthy

You know the approach

But calculation is long / calculator heavy

Might take 7–10 minutes

👉 Mark for later
Do it after finishing all easy questions.

TYPE C: Confusing / New / Tricky

You don’t understand what is being asked

Concept seems unfamiliar

Multistep combined concepts

Something seems off in the question

👉 Skip immediately
Do NOT fight with such questions.

Rule:

💥 “Chhodo aur aage badho.” (“Skip and move on.”)

This protects your time and confidence.

⭐ PART 4 — How to Solve Each Attempted Question Properly

For every question you attempt:

Copy the data neatly

Cross-check units (very important)

Write what is being asked

magnitude?

phase?

in radians or Hz?

Solve step-by-step in clean handwriting

Do a quick re-check

catches 90% calculation mistakes

This takes 3 minutes but increases accuracy drastically.

⭐ PART 5 — How Many Questions to Attempt?
IF PAPER IS EASY:

➡️ Attempt 52–55 questions

IF PAPER IS MODERATE/DIFFICULT:

➡️ Attempt 45–48 questions

This range automatically adjusts based on difficulty.

⭐ PART 6 — Ideal Time Distribution
First 90–100 minutes → Solve all easy (Type A) questions (30–35 questions)
Remaining time → Solve marked (Type B) questions
TYPE C (hard ones)

Skip unless time remains at the end.

⭐ PART 7 — The “Champion Rule”
Never take any question on ego.

Even AIR 1 students skip questions.

Your aim is high marks, not proving toughness.

⭐ PART 8 — Revision Strategy for the Last 50 Days

You must complete 2 revisions before exam.

🔵 REVISION 1: Dec 7 to Jan 10/15 (35 days)

Big subjects → 4–5 days each

Small subjects → 2–3 days each

Revise full class notes (not short notes)

Use:

highlighter

sticky notes

sparkles

Revise PYQs simultaneously

🔵 REVISION 2: Jan 15 to Jan 30 (15 days)

Big subjects → 2 days

Small subjects → 1 day

Only revise from notes + PYQs

For PYQs:

Star-marked ones → solve with pen

Simple PYQs → solve mentally within seconds

🔵 FINAL REVISION (February, 2–3 days before exam)

Do fast scan of all notes

No new PYQs

Only quick formula/concept brushing

⭐ PART 9 — Mock Test Schedule (VERY IMPORTANT)
From December to January:

👉 Give a full-length test EVERY alternate day

Example:

Dec 9: Test

Dec 11: Test

Dec 13: Test

Dec 15: Test
…and so on

You will complete 25–30 full tests, which guarantees rank improvement.

How to give the test?

Wake up

Freshen up (15 min)

Sit for 3 hours

Give the full exam in one go

Your brain must get used to 3 hours of focus.

⭐ PART 10 — Test Analysis (Max 30 minutes)

Check:

Where you made silly mistakes

Which formulas you forgot

Which topic is repeatedly weak

Which questions you wasted too much time on

Fix one problem at a time → automatic improvement.
